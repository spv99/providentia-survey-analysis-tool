Chart Description
Bargraphs A graph that presents categorical data with rectangular bars with heights proportional to the values that they represent.
Pie Charts A circular statistical graphic, that is divided into slices to illustrate numerical proportion of the population.
Box Plots A graphical way of depicting the median, maximum/minimum and quartile points, for a spread of numerical data.
Sentiment Analaysis An approach to finding the sentiment from the target population's free text responses. Each response is classified to the sentiment categories of Positive, Negative and Neutral via natural language processing.
Thematic Analaysis An approach to finding the main themes from the target population's free text responses. Each response is classified to a key topic prominent within all responses via natural language processing.
Bivariate Relationships A visual indicator of which pairs of questions are tightly correlated and vice versa.
Clustered Bar Graphs A graph that displays more than one data series in clustered horizontal columns. Each horizontal bar is grouped by category along the x-axis.
Stacked Bar Graphs A graph that displays more than one data series in clustered vertical columns. Each vertical bar is grouped by frequency along the y-axis.
Scatter Plots A graph that shows the relationship between two sets of numerical data, highlighting potential positive or negative correlations.
Sunburst Chart An overview of all responses in the data, represented by a radial ring, with each curved segment reflecting the quantitative frequency for each type of response per question.
Treemap Chart An overview of all responses in the data, represented by a collection of hierarchically structured sqaures, with each square reflecting the quantitative frequency for each type of response per question.
User Profiles A visual representation of unique user profiles, calculated by the closeness of each user's response against the others. Each user's response is signified by a point in the cluster chart, with each cluster representing a user profile.



User Profiles

Univariate analysis involves the analysis of a single variable (or column) to describe, summarise and find patterns. Bargraphs and piecharts are the two most common charts in the world of statistics and data anaysis, so for each question in your survey, we have output bargraphs and piecharts! Box plots also show univariate numerical data and can help visualise the spread of your data.

Bivariate analysis involves the study of two variables to determine the relationship between them and the strength of their correlation. To highlight the correlation between two questions, the bivariate relationships graph highlights pairs of questions that are tightly coupled. The clustered and stacked bargraphs also provide a visualise representation for each pair of question within your dataset. Similar to box plots, scatter plots displays the results of correlation and regression tests of solely numerical data in an easy-to-read manner.

Multivariate analysis occurs when three or more variables are being analysed, having multiple intercorrelations and relationships between each variable. The sunburst and treemap charts are the most interactive charts produced, allowing you to drill down into each response and visualise your respondents from a birds eye view! To find unique user profiles of your respondents the user profile chart clusters each response against each other, enabling you to identify key demographics within your survey.

To visualise and understand free text responses at a glance, sentiment and thematic analysis has also been calculated, providing you with an overview of all text-based responses in seconds! Whether you want to find out how your users are feeling or what the common themes are in the responses, the free text analysis provides a visual summary of sentiment and key topics amongst your user group!
